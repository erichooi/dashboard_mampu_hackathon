Category: Good Health and Well-Being (Data Visualization Project)


Project Objective:

To find the reason of increasing in rate of obesity for the people in Malaysia based on social media context.

Problem Statement:

Malaysia is rated as the highest Southeast Asian nations for obesity. Obesity is second behind smoking in its association with cancer. Tackling obesity as the root cause of cancer would also promote the good health and well-being of the public.

Introduction:

Malaysia has the highest rate of obesity and overweight among Asian countries with 64% of male and 65% of female population being either obese or overweight.

Ministry of Health in Malaysia and the World Health Organization (WHO)

Thus, it is important to find the reason of obesity that happened across Malaysia so that the government and private sectors can work together to reduce the rate of obesity in Malaysia.

In order to find out the reason, our team decide to analyse the food that consume most by Malaysian based on social media context, Twitter in this case.

Then, we correlated the tweet data with 2 more sources of data which are geolocation data of KFC and McDonald and price data of chicken, in hope to discover more insights about the reason that causes rate of obesity to increase in different states across Malaysia.

Dataset Used:

Obesity among Malaysian adults by state based on National Health and Morbidity Survey (dataset from MAMPU) Tweet Data (Most Mentioned Food in 2011 and 2015) Geo Data (Branch Location of KFC and McDonald Malaysia) Price Data (Chicken Price Data Crawled from pricecatcher)
